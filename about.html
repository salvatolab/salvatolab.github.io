About

Our group use cutting-edge genomics to detect and track disease-causing pathogens and their vectors/reservoirs, like mosquitoes and bats. We use genomics approaches to identify novel or re-emergent pathogens, providing crucial insights for targeted interventions and public health strategies. By providing timely emerging threats detection, we empower decision-makers to implement effective prevention, preparedness, and response measures, saving lives and mitigating the devastating impact of pandemics. Explore our site to learn more about how we safeguard public health through innovative genomic surveillance:

  • Detecting and monitoring both known and unknown pathogens in real-time
  • Unveiling the Unknown with Metagenomics
  • Studying the vectors and reservoirs of viruses
  • Developing new sequencing methods and analysis pipelines

Currently our group is based on Rio Grande do Sul (Brazil) State Health Department. Explore the SES-RS website for comprehensive public health information, resources, and news: https://saude.rs.gov.br.
