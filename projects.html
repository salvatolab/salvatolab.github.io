Projects

Genomic surveillance of zoonotic viruses using a One Health approach in Rio Grande do Sul

We are implementing (for viruses that are still poorly detected) and expand (to cover other reservoir/vector species) viral genomic surveillance in Rio Grande do Sul in the context of One Health through the genomic analysis of samples collected and diagnosed from different species of vertebrates (including humans) and invertebrates collected in the state. In addition, it is intended to characterize new pathogens of public health interest with epidemic potential from human samples from patients presenting typical symptoms of viral infection, but negative for a diagnostic panel of already known viruses. To carry out this investigation, our team process the samples using different genomic sequencing approaches based on genomic amplification, as well as metagenomics for the recovery of complete genomes of known or still unknown viral pathogens by science. The addition of the metagenomics approach and the expansion of genomic monitoring for other zoonotic viruses to the actions already regularly carried out in the surveillance programs of the state of Rio Grande do Sul represents an advance for the execution of predictive One Health surveillance, allowing for better control of the spread of known and emerging viruses, as well as assisting in the development of strategies to prevent outbreaks, epidemics, and pandemics.

Development of amplicon-based genomic sequencing and targeted metagenomics methods for detection and surveillance of viral pathogens with Epi-Pandemic potential

We are developing new cost-effective protocols for genomic sequencing for viral pathogens and detection of pathogens by directed metagenomics, to be implemented in the surveillance routine of the Rio Grande do Sul State Health Department and which may be adopted by surveillance programs of other states and countries. The addition of the metagenomic approach and the expansion of genomic monitoring will represent an advance in the execution of predictive surveillance, allowing better control of the spread of known and emerging viruses, in addition to helping in the elaboration of prevention strategies for outbreaks, epidemics, and pandemics.
